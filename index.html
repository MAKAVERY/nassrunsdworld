My Web Development Journey

Intro

The journey of web development has been far from easy. It began years ago, with many unfinished tasks and a constant battle against procrastination. The initial excitement quickly gave way to frustration as I struggled to maintain consistency. Projects would kick off with enthusiasm, only to be abandoned midway when doubts or distractions took over. Procrastination wasn’t just a bad habit—it felt like a persistent shadow, always offering excuses and slowing progress. Deadlines became fluid, goals seemed out of reach, and progress was painfully slow. Yet, through it all, there was always a spark—a reminder of why I started in the first place. That spark kept me coming back, even when it felt like I was repeatedly starting over.

FreeCodeCamp

Fortunately, FreeCodeCamp proved to be a game-changer, providing an exceptional learning platform that helped me stay on track. Its well-organized lessons and hands-on projects offered the structure and guidance I desperately needed. I set a personal goal of earning at least 200 points daily, turning my inconsistent learning habits into a steady routine. Some days were harder than others, with challenges that tested both my understanding and patience, but I remained committed. That dedication paid off when I completed the first course just before the deadline. Though it was a small achievement, it felt monumental—a powerful reminder that discipline and focus could conquer the procrastination that once held me back. FreeCodeCamp became more than just a resource; it became a solid foundation, helping me rebuild my confidence in my ability to learn and grow.

Struggles and Achievements

At first, HTML seemed simple—a language of tags and structure that appeared easy to master. But as I advanced, I realized how many gaps existed in my understanding. Concepts I thought I had mastered suddenly felt more complicated when applied in real-world situations. I had to return to the basics, revisiting each step and breaking down even the simplest elements. Although it sometimes felt repetitive, it was a necessary review that reinforced what I knew and clarified what I didn’t. With each completed section, my understanding deepened, and the process became both enlightening and rewarding. By the end, I didn’t just know HTML—I truly understood it, and that foundation became essential for everything I would learn next.

Hosting Our Work

Our lecturer later tasked us with hosting one of our FreeCodeCamp projects on a free hosting platform. Unfortunately, when the assignment came through on Saturday, I was tied up with other responsibilities. Thankfully, I had already completed the project and was able to delegate the hosting task to one of my team members. They handled it perfectly, highlighting the strength of our teamwork.

Conclusion

This journey has been both challenging and rewarding, and the support from my team has been essential to my progress.
